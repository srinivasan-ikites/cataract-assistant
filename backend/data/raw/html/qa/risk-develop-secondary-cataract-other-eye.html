  • Can I expect a secondary cataract on the other eye if it happened on the first eye?

    JUL 20, 2024

    Question:

    I had secondary cataract soon after cataract surgery on one eye and did YAG. Now that I’ve done the other eye, am I more likely to get secondary cataract again?


    Answer:

    Secondary cataract (also known as posterior capsule opacification) is when the tissue (lens capsule) that once held your eye’s natural lens becomes cloudy after cataract surgery. It is common and can take months or years to develop. In my experience what happens in one eye is similar to what happens in the other. But that is not always the case. The type of cataract, specifics about the particular surgery, and the post-operative course also play an important role and this may differ between the two eyes.

    Nonetheless, I wouldn't be overly concerned because modern YAG laser treatment (or posterior capsulotomy) is very safe and effective.


    Answered By: Gary S Hirshfield, MD
