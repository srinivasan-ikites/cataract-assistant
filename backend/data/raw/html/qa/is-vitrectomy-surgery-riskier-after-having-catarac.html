  • Is vitrectomy surgery riskier after having cataract surgery?

    SEP 16, 2022

    Question:
     Is vitrectomy surgery riskier after having cataract surgery?

    Answer:
     Vitrectomy surgery risks typically include infection, retinal tear and/or detachment, bleeding, decreased vision, and needing more or additional surgery. Prior cataract surgery typically does not increase the risks of vitrectomy surgery.

    It is important to have a complete eye exam by your ophthalmologist to evaluate the health of your eyes and to discuss the risks and benefits of vitrectomy surgery.

    Answered By: Nora Khatib, MD
