  • Can you have cataract surgery too early in the disease process?

    MAR 24, 2023

    Question:

    Is there such a thing as having cataract surgery too early in the natural history of the disease process? I may live another 30 years after cataract surgery and wonder what sort of ophthalmic advancements may occur after surgery at 65.


    Answer:

    Technology is always evolving. However, if your cataract interferes with any of your daily activities, then I would recommend surgery with the current available technology.

    Today's technology delivers excellent results that will endure. If clearer vision can improve your quality of life now, why wait another 10 years or more?


    Answered By: Gary S Hirshfield, MD

    Cataracts Surgery
