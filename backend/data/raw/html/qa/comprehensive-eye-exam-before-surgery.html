  • Why might my exam before surgery seem brief compared to others?

    OCT 04, 2024

    Question:

    Is it normal to have a less exhaustive eye exam prior to cataract surgery when compared to previous eye exams for glasses?


    Answer:

    Anyone having cataract surgery requires a comprehensive exam with the addition of several diagnostic tests.  An exam for cataract surgery should be more complete and "exhaustive" than an exam for glasses.  It is possible that your perception of the exam may not reflect what was accomplished during the visit, but if you feel that your cataract pre-op evaluation was not comprehensive, then it would be good to ask your surgeon so that they can explain the elements that went into your exam.


    Answered By: Gary S Hirshfield, MD
