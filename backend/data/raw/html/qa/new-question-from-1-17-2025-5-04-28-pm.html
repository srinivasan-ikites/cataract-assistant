  • Can I see another cataract surgeon for my other eye?

    JAN 17, 2025

    Question:

    I had a cataract removed from my right eye, but I don’t want to use the same surgeon for the other eye. Can I go to a different surgeon, and if so, which records do I need to provide?


    Answer:

    In most cases, where the surgery on the first eye was standard or uncomplicated, the new surgeon would not need much from the old record.  It would be ideal to have the power and model of the intraocular lens (IOL) used in the right eye.  If you experienced any complicating issues—either during the surgery or afterwards-- the new surgeon may want to know the details, and in that case, the entire record would be helpful. 

    Your new surgeon can decide what they might need from you—aside from the IOL and its power--after learning the details of your first procedure.


    Answered By: Gary S Hirshfield, MD
