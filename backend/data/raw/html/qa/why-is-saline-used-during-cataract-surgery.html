  • Why is saline used during cataract surgery?

    JAN 24, 2024

    Question:
     Why is saline used during cataract surgery?

    Answer:

    It’s not exactly saline but it is called balanced salt solution. There needs to be a fluid inflow during cataract surgery to keep the eye pressurized.  A saline type solution is more compatible with the existing fluid in the eye called aqueous humor.

    If one were to use sterile water with no salt then it could chemically damage the cornea. It  can also permanently damage the very sensitive endothelial cells of the cornea that are required to pump water out of the cornea to maintain its clarity.


    Answered By: Gary S Hirshfield, MD

    Cataracts Surgery
