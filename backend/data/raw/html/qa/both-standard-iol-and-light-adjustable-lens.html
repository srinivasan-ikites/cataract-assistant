  • Would a standard IOL in one eye and a light adjustable lens in the other work?

    OCT 03, 2024

    Question:

    Would a standard IOL in one eye and a light adjustable lens in the other work?


    Answer:

    Yes, this could work. Depending upon your needs, if you want the clearest distance vision the light adjustable lens (LAL) should go into the dominant eye with the goal of best distance vision. The LAL allows us to change the final power of the lens after surgery to fine tune your result.

    A standard, non-adjustable lens works very well and delivers great results, but it does not allow for adjustments after cataract surgery in a simple way like the LAL. It is very likely that you will still be satisfied with both eyes having an LAL in one and a standard, monofocal lens in the other.


    Answered By: Gary S Hirshfield, MD
