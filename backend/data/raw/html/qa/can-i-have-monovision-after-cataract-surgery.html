  • Can I keep my natural monovision after cataract surgery?

    JUN 08, 2023

    Question:

    I have natural monovision and will soon have cataract surgery done on both eyes. Will my eyes continue to be this way after surgery or do I need to put special lenses in to ensure that I continue to have this vision? I have had wonderful vision until cataracts and want to continue as I had previously after surgery.


    Answer:

    Monovision is a condition in which one eye is focused for reading and the other eye for distance. Therefore, no glasses may be needed. If you have monovision naturally, then this can likely be duplicated after cataract surgery. The surgeon can choose an intraocular lens (IOL) powered for reading vision in the eye that is currently your reading eye and an IOL for distance vision in the eye that is currently distance.


    Answered By: Gary S Hirshfield, MD
