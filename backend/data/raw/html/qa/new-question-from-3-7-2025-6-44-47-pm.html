  • Why can I see well with glasses after cataract surgery on one eye?

    MAR 07, 2025

    Question:
     I just had cataract surgery on my left (dominant) eye with a lens for distant vision. I'm wearing my old glasses (-4.00 in each lens) and I can see distance objects very well, even though the left eye has a distance lens implanted. Why is that? I thought the left eye wouldn't be able to function with the glasses.

    Answer:

    You might be suppressing the distance vision in your operated eye while wearing your glasses and using your unoperated right eye for distance.

    If your distance vision is fine without glasses, you might consider setting the right eye for near vision (called monovision) if you have cataract surgery in this eye in the future.  However, if your distance vision without glasses is not great, then the right eye should be set for distance vision as well, leaving you more dependent on reading glasses for near vision with both eyes.

    It is somewhat unusual (although not impossible) to suppress the dominant eye when viewing distance, and this suggests that perhaps your right eye is actually dominant, or that you don't have a heavily weighted dominant eye.


    Answered By: Gary S Hirshfield, MD

    Cataracts
