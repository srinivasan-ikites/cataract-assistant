  • Can I expect to have similar distance vision without glasses after cataract surgery?

    MAR 25, 2024

    Question:

    I am nearsighted with glasses and am considering near vision monofocal IOLs in both eyes. This will allow me to see up close without glasses but continue to wear glasses for distance. Can I expect my distance vision without glasses after cataract surgery to be similar to what it is today?


    Answer:

    It is very likely you will see better at distance without glasses after cataract surgery. It depends on the severity of your cataracts and the degree of your nearsightedness before surgery.  

    For example, if you are -7.00 and after cataract surgery you are -2.50 you will see much better without glasses post-op. If you are -1.50 and end up -2.50 after surgery, you may still see better even with increased nearsightedness because your IOL (artificial intraocular lens) will be clearer than your cataract. It is important to talk with your ophthalmologist prior to surgery to discuss your expectations and visual goals.  Your surgeon will be able to best answer your questions based on your current eye exam.


    Answered By: Gary S Hirshfield, MD
