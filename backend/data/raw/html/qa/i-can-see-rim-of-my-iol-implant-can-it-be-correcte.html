  • I can see the rim of my IOL implant—can it be corrected?

    APR 09, 2016

    Question:

    I've had an IOL (artificial intraocular lens placed in eye after cataract surgery) in my left eye for over 30 years now. Just started to see the rim of the implant along with blurriness. It’s really bad when driving at night. Do IOLs slide off the eyes? Is there anything that can be done to correct it?


    Answer:

    While it is uncommon, IOLs can shift in position and this can produce a combination of symptoms including blurriness and glare. Fortunately, your IOL should never slide out of your eye, but if the symptoms become troublesome, there are surgical procedures to reposition and more steadfastly hold an IOL in place. Talking with an experienced cataract surgeon, preferably the one who performed your procedure, would be a good place to begin.


    Answered By: James M Heltzer, MD

    Surgery Cataracts
