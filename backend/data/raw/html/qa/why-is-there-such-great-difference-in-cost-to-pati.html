  • Why is there such a great difference in cost to patient between putting in regular lens for cataracts versus toric for astigmatism?

    AUG 08, 2016

    Question:

    Why is there such a great difference in cost to patient between putting in regular lens for cataracts versus toric for astigmatism? It seems to be same operation.


    Answer:

    The issue is that the lens itself is more expensive by many times and is not reimbursed by insurance. Plus, more pre-operative measurements and calculations are needed and the follow-up after surgery can be more involved. Even the surgery itself can be different: The capsulotomy (or opening the anterior lens capsule) is lens specific. The eye has to be specifically marked for the axis correction of the toric lens, and sometimes an aberrometer (a special device that measures the way light travels through your eye) is used to make sure the lens is correctly placed.

    Though the operation is very similar, there are some important differences that do add cost to the procedure.


    Answered By: Jeffrey Whitman, MD
