  • Why are multifocal IOLs not recommended for me?

    MAR 10, 2022

    Question:
     I'm 64 and a monofocal lens is recommended due to dense cataracts and destabilizing supporting structures inside the eye. Is this a good reason not to go with multifocal or accommodative lenses? How does the multifocal lens differ from the monofocal, with respect to the supporting structures of the eye?

    Answer:
     Multifocal lenses must be placed in a secure capsular bag (or lens capsule, the thin membrane securing the eye’s natural lens that’s left intact after surgery to hold the IOL) and well-centered. If the “supporting structures” (presumably of the capsular bag) are inadequate, then a multifocal IOL may not be the best idea. Your surgeon may also be concerned about the lens becoming dislocated and moving in ensuing years. Small dislocations may create significantly more symptoms in a patient with a multifocal lens.

    Answered By: Gary S Hirshfield, MD

    Cataracts
