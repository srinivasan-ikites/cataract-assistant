  • Can I choose not to have an IOL?

    FEB 17, 2025

    Question:

    Can one have a cataract removed without correcting vision? I am nearsighted with astigmatism but do a lot of close-up work. I don't mind wearing glasses and like being able to take off my glasses to see very up close and want to avoid the potential side effects of IOLs.


    Answer:

    An important part of the discussion that should take place with your ophthalmologist before cataract surgery is how you would like to have your vision corrected. This includes the different intraocular lenses (or IOLs) that may be available to you, as well as the refractive target, which in your case is to remain nearsighted. A helpful way to think about this is where you would like your vision to have the best focus and how clear you would like it to be without glasses.

    An experienced ophthalmologist should be able to make recommendations with your preferences in mind.


    Answered By: Kurt Kelley MD
