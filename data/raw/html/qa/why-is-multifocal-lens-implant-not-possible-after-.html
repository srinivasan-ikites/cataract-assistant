  • Why is a multifocal lens implant not possible after vitrectomy/scleral buckle surgery?

    NOV 22, 2016

    Question:

    Why is a multifocal lens implant not possible after vitrectomy and scleral buckle surgery (surgery that helps reattach the retina, or light sensitive tissue, to the back of the eye)? I will be having a cataract removed (surgery to replace eye’s cloudy natural lens with clear artificial lens) eventually and might prefer this to monovision.


    Answer:

    This can depend on a number of things. If the macula (center of the retina) was damaged by the retinal detachment or hole, multifocal lenses are not recommended as they can actually make vision worse since they decrease the amount of incoming light. Also, if there was damage to the posterior capsule of the lens during the vitrectomy, there would not be a support structure to hold this type of lens in place. It is best to ask your surgeon what he recommends and why.


    Answered By: Jeffrey Whitman, MD

    Cataracts Surgery
