  • Is it possible to have a successful cataract surgery with pseudoexfoliation?

    MAR 24, 2017

    Question:

    I have psuedoexfoliation (when flaky dandruff-like deposits peel off the lens inside the eye and potentially block fluid from leaving the eye) and need to have cataract surgery. Is it possible to have a successful surgery with this condition? The ophthalmologist wasn't very encouraging about my problem. I wasn't aware of it until I had an appointment to schedule cataract surgery.


    Answer:

    Most patients with pseudoexfoliation have no problems with the surgery and if you do, there are corrective actions that can be taken during the surgery to produce a good outcome. If you surgeon does not feel comfortable with such surgery, you are entitled to get a second opinion.


    Answered By: Jeffrey Whitman, MD

    Surgery Cataracts
