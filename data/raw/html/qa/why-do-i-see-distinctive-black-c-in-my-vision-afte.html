  • Why do I see a distinctive black “C” in my vision after having a toric lens implanted?

    JUL 22, 2016

    Question:

    I had a toric intraocular lens implanted which corrected my astigmatism and cataract in my left eye. My vision is terrific but it looks like I'm wearing a contact lens that has a distinctive black “C” on it. It does not affect my vision, but it does not seem "normal." It almost seems like someone took a black sharpie marker and drew on the left edge of the lens. Do I need a replacement?


    Answer:

    What you are describing is known as a negative dysphotopsia—an unusual reflection image. Replacement does not always fix the problem, but time usually makes it go away. Another option can be the insertion of what is called a piggyback lens which can negate the image. Talk to your surgeon about the options.


    Answered By: Jeffrey Whitman, MD

    Surgery Cataracts
