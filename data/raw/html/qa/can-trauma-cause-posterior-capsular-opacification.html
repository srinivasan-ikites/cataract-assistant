  • Can trauma cause posterior capsular opacification?

    NOV 10, 2022

    Question:
     Can head trauma or falling and hitting your head cause a posterior capsular opacification?

    Answer:

    Posterior capsular opacification (or PCO, and sometimes referred to as a “secondary cataract”) is a harmless clouding of the thin membrane or lens capsule that supports the new artificial lens after cataract surgery. While it is not completely understood why some people get this and some do not, it is safe to say that trauma is not a cause. If you have cloudy vision from a PCO, a posterior capsulotomy restores clear vision by making an opening in the lens capsule.

    If you are having vision issues after hitting your head, please consult your primary care physician or ophthalmologist immediately.


    Answered By: Kurt Kelley MD
