  • Can an IOL become damaged once inside the eye?

    JAN 30, 2024

    Question:

    I had cataract surgery about a year and a half ago. Can the IOL become damaged or scratched once inside the eye?


    Answer:

    Once your IOL (or artificial intraocular lens) has been placed inside of your eye there is very little chance that any damage can occur to it, especially a year and a half after surgery.

    There are a few very uncommon conditions that can harm your implant, but they are very unlikely. First, the treatment of a cloudy membrane (secondary cataract) with a YAG laser (called a posterior capsulotomy) rarely produces slight imperfections in the lens, most of which do not affect the quality of your vision. Still more uncommon, the IOL can be squeezed from its normal shape and position by abnormal contraction of the bag or sac into which the lens has been implanted. Even more uncommon is severe trauma to the implanted eye as in an auto accident.

    If you have any specific concerns I would suggest you follow up with your surgeon, but I think you will continue to do fine.

    This question was originally answered on Dec. 21, 2016.


    Answered By: Wayne F Bizer DO

    Cataracts
