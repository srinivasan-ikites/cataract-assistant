  • Can multifocal IOLs be used with strabismus?

    JAN 08, 2025

    Question:

    Can multifocal IOLs be used with strabismus?


    Answer:

    I would say it is best to avoid a multifocal IOL (intraocular lens) with strabismus (misaligned or crossed eyes). This could be a problem because sometimes correcting the patient’s vision with an IOL can make the patient see double if their eyes are still misaligned after cataract surgery. If you end up needing strabismus surgery, it could make that procedure more complicated as well.

    Please consult with both your cataract surgeon and your strabismus specialist so they can help you choose the IOL that is right for your specific situation.


    Answered By: Stacey J Kruger, MD
