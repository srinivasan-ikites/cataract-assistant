  • Why am I nauseous after cataract surgery?

    JAN 22, 2024

    Question:

    I had cataract surgery with a monofocal lens implant placed in my left eye three weeks ago. Since then I have been disoriented and nauseous. My right eye is almost 20/20 and I have not done surgery in that eye yet. I had to get stronger reading glasses after the surgery. What is making me so nauseous? 


    Answer:

    The difference in correction from the distance eye to the now near eye may be a bit much for you to handle — about two diopters is all that most can tolerate. See where you are with your ophthalmologist and if there is too much difference, consider LASIK to bring the eyes a bit closer together (less nearsighted in the monofocal IOL near eye).

    This question was originally answered on Mar. 10, 2014.


    Answered By: Jeffrey Whitman, MD

    Cataracts Surgery
